Voice

Plainspoken, warm, never salesy.

The voice should read like Tyler talking across a kitchen table — casual, honest, quietly confident about the craft. This holds across all three ICP tiers; only the emphasis shifts slightly, not the voice itself.

DoTalk like a person: "We'll get that panel sorted before dinner."
Don'tSound corporate: "We provide comprehensive electrical solutions."
DoLet the detail-obsession show: mention the small things others skip.
Don'tOversell with urgency language or fake scarcity.
DoUse humor sparingly, dry and warm — never at the customer's expense.
Don'tGet technical without translating it back to what it means for them.
DoJustify the price with what it buys (reliability, mastery) — stated plainly, not defensively.
Don'tApologize for the price, or compare against cheaper competitors.

Voice Across the ICP Ladder

Same voice, different emphasis.

The words don't change register between tiers — but which part of the message leads can shift by context (e.g. which service pages or ad copy a client is likely to land on).

Luxury Estate

Leads with mastery, discretion, and flawless finish — the "I'm rich!" feeling comes from work that's simply never a problem, not from luxury language.

Established Affluent

Leads with reliability and relationship — "the electrician we call every time," steady and unremarkable in the best way.

Growing Family

Leads with warmth and straightforwardness — approachable, honest pricing, a partner as they build out their home and their life.
