Client Journey

From first hello to the next referral.

Every stage is a chance to prove both halves of the brand — the warmth and the precision — at once. The "what the site needs" tag on each stage is what carries directly into the Website Menu.

Stage 1

Discovery

"Can I actually trust these people?"

A neighbor's referral, a social post, or a local search. The website and reviews need to answer that trust question before the phone ever rings.

Site needs: trust signals above the fold
Stage 2

Quote Call

"Are they actually listening to my problem?"

The standardized intake script captures the right details warmly — not like a form being read off a screen.

Site needs: one obvious way to request a quote
Stage 3

Scheduled Visit

"Will they actually show up when they said?"

Confirmation and reminders that feel personal, backed by a Housecall Pro system that never drops the appointment.

Site needs: booking flow tied to Housecall Pro
Stage 4

Job Day

"Is this actually as good as people say?"

The moment the brand is made real — clean work, small details handled, the "family" feeling delivered in person.

Site needs: real before/after proof, not stock photos
Stage 5

Invoice & Follow-Up

"Was that as easy as the rest of it?"

A clean, no-surprises invoice through Housecall Pro, plus a simple ask: a review, or to keep Griggs in mind next time.

Site needs: nothing — this stage lives in Housecall Pro
Stage 6

Referral Loop

"Who else needs to hear about this?"

A happy customer becomes the next neighbor's referral — the same growth engine that built the business, now systemized instead of left to chance.

Site needs: reviews surfaced prominently, easy to share
What This Means for the Site

Three real requirements, not guesses.

Trust signals before the call, one obvious booking path, and Housecall Pro integration are no longer nice-to-haves — they're the direct output of where customers actually get stuck or reassured. These carry straight into the Website Menu next.
